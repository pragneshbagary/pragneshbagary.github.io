Alignment fingerprints: a per-dimension decomposition of reward alignment

A single alignment score tells a reward designer that something is wrong, but not what. Decomposing the TAC metric into exact per-dimension Shapley attributions localizes sign errors the scalar cannot register, and the experiments map where the diagnostic works, where it fails silently under feature correlation, and what it can never detect.

Replicating Soft-TAC: a reward-learning experiment on LunarLander

A from-scratch reimplementation of a 2026 RL paper's central claim - that a tanh-based Soft-TAC loss degrades more gracefully than cross-entropy under label noise. Building a synthetic ground truth let me measure reward recovery directly, and a careful noise sweep revealed how much of the effect was real, how much was seed variance, and how much was feature scale.
